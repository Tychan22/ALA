What the Data Shows

📍

Open Candle = Bias Signal

Every session, the 9:30 open candle revealed direction. A sweep + rejection of the BSL (high) = bearish day. A bounce off SSL (low) = bullish day. This was correct 7 out of 8 observed sessions.

🎯

Both Levels Get Hit

5 of 8 observed days saw price sweep one level then reverse to the other. The midpoint frequently acted as the inflection point — a rejection at midpoint after a sweep often confirmed continuation.

📐

Midpoint = Key S/R

Midpoint hit = partial W. On the March 12 and March 9 sessions, hitting the midpoint was meaningful — it acted as real support/resistance, not just a reference line.

⚠️

Beware Momentum Continuations

Two losses came from shorting into strong upside momentum after a high-volume candle. When a session rips +230pts and price is above EMA by 160+pts, don't fade it — wait for structure to form.

What to Improve

1

Add Sweep Confirmation Before Entry

Don't enter the moment price touches BSL/SSL. Wait for the candle to close back inside the range — that closing candle confirms the sweep and rejection. This filters false breakouts and gives a cleaner entry with tighter stop.

2

High-Volume Candles = Trap Warning

When a single bar sweeps both BSL and SSL on massive volume (2x+ average), it's accumulation/distribution — not a fade signal. Wait for the new direction to establish before entering. Both 1H losses had this pattern.

3

Don't Short Into Strong Upside Momentum

If price is 150+pts above the EMA band and the session is up +230pts, the draw on liquidity is likely higher — not lower. Respect the trend until EMA is recaptured from above as resistance.

4

Use Midpoint as Partial Target / BE

On the SSL → BSL trade (or vice versa), take 50% off at the midpoint and move stop to breakeven. Validated on March 9, March 12, and March 16 where midpoint was a real inflection.

5

EMA Band = Regime Filter

Price above EMA band = bullish regime, look for longs. Price below EMA = bearish, look for shorts. Only fade moves when price sweeps a level AND EMA confirms the rejection direction.

6

Avoid Entries After 12 PM EDT

Several sessions showed price drifting aimlessly after noon. The draw on liquidity concept works best in the NY AM session (9:30–12:00). Levels hit after 12 PM tend to be chop, not clean sweep-and-reverse setups.
